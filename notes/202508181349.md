## Leftover string:

(ยง) An old blog post by Rafa Irizarry tells us that [lowering the GWAS threshold would save millions of
dollars](https://web.archive.org/web/20201112012243/simplystatistics.org/2017/06/20/lowering-the-gwas-threshold-would-save-millions-of-dollars/).
The point: if you accept a larger false discovery rate you get more statistical
power. It is clear that you can trade type I error for type II error and for
expensive genomics procedures perhaps the odd "false discovery" is cheaper than
sequencing more samples. Don't think that this particular concern is too
important almost 10 years later, but it is an interesting question in general:
do you want to spend money or shoeleather?
